Property Insurance 101

What are the types of homeowner's insurance?

The types of homeowner's insurance are basic, broad (which covers everything that basic does as well as building collapse and damage done to heating, air conditioning, plumbing and sprinkler systems), and special form.

What factors affect homeowner's insurance costs?

Location, condition, type of weather that occurs in your area, construction materials, and size.

What isn't covered by homeowner's and renter's insurance?

Damage caused by flooding and very expensive items (since most insurance policies only cover valubles up to a certain limit).

Renter/Condominium Information

What does condominium insurance cover?

Damage due to smoke, windstorm, hail, fire, lightning, earthquakes, theft, vandalism, water escape, as well as personal property coverage and personal liability.

What does renter's insurance cover?

Theft, loss of personal property, and loss of use.
